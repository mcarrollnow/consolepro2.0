FULL CHEMICAL NAME

The full chemical name of AOD-9604 is Tyr-Leu-Arg-Ile-Val-Gln-Cys-Arg-Ser-Val-Glu-Gly-Ser-Cys-Gly-Phe, corresponding to the amino acid sequence 177-191 of human growth hormone (hGH). This sequence includes tyrosine (Tyr) at the N-terminus, enhancing stability and bioactivity, followed by leucine (Leu), arginine (Arg), isoleucine (Ile), valine (Val), glutamine (Gln), cysteine (Cys), serine (Ser), glutamic acid (Glu), glycine (Gly), and phenylalanine (Phe). The peptides structure is linear, with disulfide bonds potentially forming between cysteine residues, contributing to its lipolytic function without stimulating IGF-1 production, distinguishing it from full hGH.

ALIASES

Yes, AOD-9604 is also referred to by its synonym Tyr-hGH(177-191), which denotes its origin as the tyrosine-modified C-terminal fragment (amino acids 177-191) of human growth hormone. It may occasionally be listed as hGH Fragment 177-191 in scientific literature or research databases, emphasizing its structural derivation. No other widely recognized aliases or synonyms have been documented, maintaining Tyr-hGH(177-191) as the standard alternative designation in preclinical research.

PREPARATION INSTRUCTIONS

In preclinical studies, AOD-9604 has demonstrated quantifiable efficacy in reducing body fat. Human trials (Heffernan et al., 2001) reported a significant reduction in visceral and subcutaneous fat (up to 15% in obese individuals) after 12 weeks at doses of 450 g/day, with no significant increase in IGF-1 levels, confirming its separation from hGH growth effects. Animal studies in obese rats showed similar fat mass reductions (10-20%) without appetite suppression, focusing on lipolysis enhancement.

Mechanism of Action

AOD-9604s mechanism of action involves mimicking the lipolytic region of human growth hormone (hGH), specifically the C-terminal fragment 177-191, to enhance fat breakdown (lipolysis) and inhibit fat storage (lipogenesis) in adipose tissue. It activates -adrenergic pathways and PPAR signaling, promoting free fatty acid release without stimulating IGF-1 or growth effects, distinguishing it from full hGH.

No renal, cardiac, or neurological adverse effects were noted, and safety profiles remain favorable within studied ranges, though long-term data is limited.
